Thoughts on Severance (Season 2)

I went into season two of Severance skeptical. The first season was beautifully shot and full of ideas, but it spent a lot of time building a mystery box without paying much off. By the end I was frustrated more than hooked.

Season two fixes almost everything I had a problem with.

what works

The show finally starts answering questions. Not all of them — it's still Severance, it's not going to hand you everything — but enough that the mystery feels earned rather than strung along. The world gets bigger in ways that feel logical rather than random.

The cast is still exceptional. Adam Scott in particular does something interesting with the two versions of his character that I didn't expect. There's a scene in the fourth episode I won't spoil that I've thought about every day since I watched it.

what doesn't work

A few side characters get storylines that go nowhere for most of the season. The pacing is still uneven — some episodes feel like they're killing time. And the finale, while good, sets up a third season in a way that made me feel the familiar season-one frustration starting to creep back.

verdict

Worth watching, especially if season one left you cold. It's a genuine improvement. Whether the show sticks the landing overall depends entirely on whether they can close it out — which remains to be seen.

Recommend: yes, with the caveat that you'll need to watch season one first and it takes a few episodes to get going.
