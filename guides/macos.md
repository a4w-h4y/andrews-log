macOS is capable of a lot more than most people use it for. The defaults are
fine, but a handful of changes — to habits more than settings — make a
meaningful difference in day-to-day speed and comfort.

### Keyboard over mouse

The single highest-return habit on macOS is learning to keep your hands on
the keyboard. Every round trip to the mouse costs time. Spotlight
(`Cmd + Space`) alone replaces most reasons to touch the Dock.
App switching with `Cmd + Tab` replaces most reasons to click
the menu bar. The rest follows from there.

See: [Mac keyboard shortcuts I use every single day](../posts/tip-mac-shortcuts.html)

### Window management

macOS doesn't have great built-in window snapping, but you don't need a
third-party app to get by. `Ctrl + Left/Right Arrow` moves you
between Spaces (virtual desktops), and you can assign apps to specific
Spaces so your layout is always the same. For more control, Rectangle
(free) adds keyboard-driven snapping without the overhead of something
like Magnet.

### Spotlight as a calculator and converter

Most people use Spotlight to launch apps. It also does unit conversions,
currency, and arithmetic. Type `15% of 84` or `45 miles in km` directly
into Spotlight and get the answer without opening a browser.

### Hidden settings worth knowing

A few things that aren't obvious from the System Settings UI:

- Key repeat speed can be set faster than the UI slider allows via Terminal.
- The Dock auto-hide delay can be reduced to near-zero the same way.
- Holding `Option` while clicking the menu bar Wi-Fi icon shows detailed network diagnostics.
- `Cmd + Shift + .` toggles hidden files visible in Finder.
